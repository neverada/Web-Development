Heart of Onion

by Neverree • form: free verse

Some hearts are made of gold, Some of steel, some of rose — So soft, only God knows. But I have a heart of onion.
It might sound silly to you, But I’m not lying. You’ll peel through my layers — And you’ll end up crying.
My layers are intricately designed. They only guard me from so much pain. There’s so much ache yet to contain In layers of an onion that quietly remain.
And as I speak, you hold a knife — The silver on it glistens so bright It nearly blinds my sight. But dull blades can’t cut what I’ve kept.
Sharp knives have aced What dull blades hoped to do. I have a bittersweet taste — Still, you need me in every stew.
And when the knife glides through me, Your eyes sting with unfathomable tears. You hate me, but you need me, dear — And I hate that each of my layers still care.
Despite all this, I love my heart of onion. It might sound silly — alright, I know. But I think my heart of onion is better than Hearts of rose, gold, or steel, though.
Steel’s impenetrable, gold only glitters. Roses? They’re delicate — they’re everywhere. But a heart of onion, I suppose, Is rare to find and harder to dispose.
